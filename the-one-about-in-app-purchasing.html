The One About In-App Purchasing

There appears to have some confusion on how our in-app purchasing works. I guess that means we're not doing it like everyone else, and I think that's a good thing.

In-App Purchasing Confusion

It's been a couple weeks now since we launched and I have definitely heard the in-app purchasing (IAP) we have is confusing.

In our next release, I will certainly be changing the wording on it to help clarify how it works. It appears that potential players think that you have to spend $40 just to unlock the campaign and get some tiles. That's just not the case. We've got several hours of free campaign gameplay in there plus as much pass-and-play multiplayer as you want.

As an F2P designer, I can honestly say I HATE when games are free, but they make it so hard or impossible to play that you have to buy in-game currency. That is an awful bait-and-switch and I won't have it.

There are three cardinal rules (in my opinion) on free-to-play game design that must be followed.

  1. You must not enable "Pay for Power" in multiplayer games
  2. You must not sell an unfinishable, crippled game
  3. Any IAP should either enhance the player's experience or save the player time

So, I think the word "Shortcut" instead of "Unlock" might be the key there.

How the IAP Actually Works

The IAP basically comes in two flavors: random tile packs and campaign shortcuts.

The random tile packs operate like standard CCG card packs, but with tiles. In the most basic pack, you'll get three commons and one uncommon. The uncommon having a 25% chance of being a rare. From there, they differ in price-per-tile, commonality, and faction.

The campaign shortcuts (previously called unlocks!) are really just ways to skip all the randomness, while skipping ahead in the campaign story to the harder stages. With these unlocks, you get all the tiles earned normally during that campaign chapter, plus, you'll skip to the next campaign chapter.

But, as I stated in my 3 cardinal rules, you don't need to buy anything to play through the entire campaign while earning enough tiles to make full, playable tilesets. In fact, you earn all the tiles (I've created so far) in the game by playing through the first 3 of the 6 available chapters in the campaign.
